Hoysala Temple Architecture

An Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) Perspective

Historical Background

Hoysala temple architecture developed between the 11th and 14th centuries in present-day Karnataka under the Hoysala dynasty. These temples represent a unique blend of artistic excellence, scientific planning, and spiritual philosophy.

Architectural Features

  • Star-shaped (stellate) temple plan
  • Soapstone (chloritic schist) material
  • Highly detailed wall carvings
  • Lathe-turned polished pillars

Famous Hoysala Temples

Chennakesava Temple

Located in Belur, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, built in 1117 CE.

Hoysaleswara Temple

Located in Halebidu, dedicated to Lord Shiva with intricate sculptures.

Kesava Temple

Located in Somanathapura, known for its perfect star-shaped plan.

Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) Relevance

Hoysala temples showcase indigenous Indian knowledge in geometry, material science, structural engineering, sculpture, and spirituality. They were constructed without modern machinery, cement, or steel.
